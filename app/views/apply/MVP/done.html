Application sent

Your reference number is your National Insurance number

What happens next

Someone from your local jobcentre will contact you to arrange an appointment, if they have not already.

At your appointment you will be asked to:

  • prove your identity
  • agree what you need to do in return for getting New Style ESA
  • give evidence to support your application

Things to bring to your appointment

Identity documents

You will need to bring 3 identity documents with you:

  • 1 current official identity document, such as a passport, driving licence or residency card
  • 2 original documents in your name, such as a utility bill, bank statement, hospital letter or birth certificate
Help with identity documents

Bring 1 of the following official identity documents:

  • UK passport
  • EEA passport
  • UK photo driving licence
  • national identity card
  • residence permit or card
  • immigration status or document
  • registration or naturalisation certificate

Bring 2 originals or official copies of the following documents:

  • birth certificate
  • recent bank or building society statement
  • Council Tax statement
  • NHS medical card
  • debit or charge card
  • expired passport
  • expired full or provisional driving licence
  • Local Authority rent card
  • marriage or civil partnership certificate
  • tenancy agreement

Supporting evidence

Based on your application, you will also need to bring:

  • fit notes (you might know these as sick notes)
  • SSP1 form, if you have one
  • P45 form, if you have one
  • your 5 most recent weekly pay slips from Co-op
  • your 2 most recent monthly pay slips from Boots

You might be asked for additional pay slips if your earnings vary.

If you need to change your appointment

Call 0800 328 5644 if you need to change your appointment.

Lines are open Monday to Friday, 8am to 6pm and are closed on public holidays.
