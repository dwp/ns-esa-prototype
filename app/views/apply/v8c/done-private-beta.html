You’ve completed the first part of your application

What happens now

Someone from your local jobcentre will contact you to arrange an appointment, if they have not already.

At your appointment you will be asked to:

  • prove your identity
  • agree what you need to do in return for getting 'new style' Employment and Support Allowance.
  • give any evidence to support your application

Things to bring to your appointment

You’ll need to bring the following to your appointment:

  • 3 identity documents
  • fit notes (you might know these as sick notes)
  • SSP1 form (this confirms when your Statutory Sick Pay ends)
  • P45 form
  • your 5 most recent weekly pay slips from Co-op
  • your 2 most recent monthly pay slips from Boots
  • proof of any pensions
  • proof of any permanent health insurance

You might be asked for additional pay slips if your earnings vary.

What you need to prove your identity

You need to bring 3 identity documents with you:

  • your current passport, driving licence or bank card
  • 2 documents that prove your name and address, such as a utility bill, bank statement, hospital letter or birth certificate

If you need to change your appointment

Call 0800 328 5644 if you need to change your appointment.

Lines are open Monday to Friday, 8am to 6pm and closed on public holidays.
