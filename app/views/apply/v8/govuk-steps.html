How to claim 'new style' Employment and Support Allowance (ESA): step by step

You might be eligible for ‘new style’ ESA if you have a disability or health condition that affects how much you can work.

  1. Step 1 : Check you're eligible

    Check what type of ESA you should apply for.

    Check if you're eligible

    You might also be eligible for Universal Credit.

    Find out about Universal Credit

  2. Step 2 : Start your claim and arrange your interview

    Apply for 'new style' ESA online

    You'll arrange an interview with your work coach after you submit your application. It will be within 10 working days. You'll be told what to bring.

    You'll be sent a UCESA1 form. Fill it in before your interview.

  3. Step 3 : Attend the interview with your work coach

    You'll agree what actions you'll need to take to get payments. This is called a 'Claimant Commitment'.

  4. Step 4 : Get your first payment

    You’ll get a letter telling you if you’ll get ‘new style’ ESA and how much.

    How much you're paid

    You'll get your first payment within 2 weeks of your interview.

  5. and Give updates about your health and circumstances

    To keep getting 'new style' ESA you must send fit notes regularly, report any change in your circumstances and keep to the commitment you signed at your interview.

    Send a fit note

    Report any change in your circumstances

  6. Step 5 : Fill in and send the ESA50 form

    You'll get the ESA50 form in the post within 4 weeks of your first payment. Return it within 28 days.

    Get help filling in the ESA50 form

  7. Step 6 : Attend your Work Capability Assessment

    You'll usually need to go for a Work Capability Assessment. If you do, you'll get a letter telling you where to go and when.

    Prepare for your Work Capability Assessment

  8. Step 7 : Get a decision about how much you'll get

    You'll get a letter with a decision. It will also tell you if you'll need to be assessed again.

    How much you can get

    Find out how to challenge the decision

    To keep getting the right amount you'll need to report any change in your circumstances.

    Report a change of circumstances

  9. Step 8 : Check if your payments will stop or if you'll be reassessed

    You'll be told if your payments will stop and when.

    Get support from Jobcentre Plus when your payments stop

    If you'll need to be assessed again, you'll be sent an ESA50 form. This is normally within 3 years.
