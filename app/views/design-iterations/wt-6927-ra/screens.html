Version 2.0: Reasonable adjustments

Problem statement

The current reasonable adjustment question on the NSESA online application is a simple yes-no radio question, with no further option to specify. This is causing confusion for users. Some users mistakenly believe they must select ‘yes’ in order to be eligible for NSESA. As a result, many users indicate they need a reasonable adjustment when they don’t. This triggers unnecessary outbound calls for Service centre agents, as every ‘yes’ must be followed up during processing.

This leads to a higher workload to agents who need to make redundant outbound calls, delays in identifying and supporting claimants who genuinely need reasonable adjustments , and also a poor user experience for claimants who feel uncertain or mislead by the current question.

We aim to improve the question’s clarity and format to help users understand what we mean by reasonable adjustments, so that they can select only what applies to them.

Hypothesis

We believe that by replacing the current yes-no radio question with clearer content making clear this is about adjustments for appointments and moving the question to the end of the application, will achieve that users better understand what we mean by reasonable adjustments, and only those who need it support will make a selection.

We will know this when:

  • fewer users select a reasonable adjustment by mistake
  • fewer unnecessary outbound call are made by SCAs
  • we see improved support for users who need a reasonable adjustment

We believe this will ultimately result in a more inclusive service, increased user satisfaction and compliance with legal accessibility requirements.
