What happens next

Send us medical evidence to support your application

{# BAU Evidence #}

You need to provide medical evidence from the start of your claim. You have asked for your claim to start on ((claim start date)).

You can send us a fit note (also called a sick note or a statement of fitness for work) using the Send Your Fit Note service (opens in new tab).


Send us another type of medical evidence

As well as fit notes, we also accept these medical certificates:

  • Med10 (if you're in hospital for treatment that lasts more than 7 days)
  • MATB1 (if you're pregnant but cannot get Statutory Maternity Pay or Maternity Allowance)
  • CF73 (if you've been admitted to a psychiatric hospital)
  • SR1 (a form completed and sent to us by a doctor, consultant or specialist nurse. This has replaced the DS1500 form)

Other forms of evidence might include a doctor's statement, consultant's report or Local Authority notice. 

Send your medical evidence to:

Freepost DWP ESA 26

Medical evidence always needs to be in date. When your fit note or certificate expires, you need to get and send us a new one. We will tell you when you can stop sending it.

Claimant Commitment appointment

We will aim to contact you by ((contact date)) to book your 'Claimant Commitment' appointment. This is usually held over the phone.

During this appointment we will ask you to:

  • confirm your identity
  • agree to tell us if your circumstances change
  • accept an agreement that sets out what you can expect of us, and what we expect of you. This is called a Claimant Commitment

We may also ask some questions about your health to help us understand your situation. You will not need to give us a detailed medical history at this appointment. We will ask for this at a later stage.

You need to complete this appointment before you can get New Style ESA.

Report a change of circumstances

If your circumstances change, for example you change your address, you need to let us know (opens in a new tab).
